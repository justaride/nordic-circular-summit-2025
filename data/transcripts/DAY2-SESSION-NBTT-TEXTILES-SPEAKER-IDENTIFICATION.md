## SESSION OVERVIEW

### Context
Launch session for the Nordic Baltic Textile Transition Group, held as part of Day 2 of the Nordic Circular Summit 2025. Originally launched in Helsinki (2024), this relaunch expands to include Baltic countries (Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania) alongside Nordic nations.

### Key Topics Covered
1. **Purpose and Structure**: Multi-stakeholder platform for textile circular transition
2. **Regional Challenges**: Used textile management across Nordic-Baltic region
3. **Danish Model**: Voluntary Sector Collaboration on Textiles as inspiration
4. **EPR Implementation**: Extended Producer Responsibility harmonization needs
5. **Infrastructure**: Sorting, reuse, recycling capacity mapping
6. **Next Steps**: Two online seminars + one physical event format

### Key Frameworks Referenced
- EU Textile Strategy
- Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)
- Digital Product Passport (DPP)
- Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR)
- UK Textiles Pact / WRAP Textile 2030
- Textile Action Network (RAP)

### Regional Value Chain Analysis
- **Nordics**: Lead in design, R&D, automated sorting, recycling technology (chemical & mechanical)
- **Baltics**: Strong in manual sorting for reuse, production capacity, repair expertise
- **Shared Challenges**: Collection bottlenecks, contamination, volatile export markets, insufficient high-value recycling

- Baltic Circular Hotspot
