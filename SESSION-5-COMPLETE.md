## Key Themes & Insights

### 1. Place Economy Principles
- Triangulating place + approach + topic for concrete value propositions
- Economic language as essential for sustainability communication
- Value chains connecting to market/value spheres of location
- Trust as the most important infrastructure for place development

### 2. Riga's Circular Transformation
- Achieved EU Mission Label (1 of 100 climate-neutral smart cities)
- 8 thematic areas: waste, energy, mobility, transport, culture, business, etc.
- 3 work directions: citizen engagement, municipality resources, circular businesses
- Created first urban resource center as community hub + repair facility

### 3. Cesis Bioregion (First in Baltics)
- Memorandum of goodwill with 13 institutions (1 year to craft)
- 6 principles, 8 working directions
- Focus on locally produced organic food in public institutions
- Waste → circular economy center transformation
- Integrated bioregion principles into LEADER program funding

### 4. Arctic Construction Wisdom
- Traditional knowledge: "As a WIC, we can use almost anything"
- Robust solutions for remote areas with limited maintenance access
- Population context: Greenland = 0.00068% of world population
- High detail required, adaptable applications of available materials
- Arctic approaches may inform other sparse-population regions

### 5. Nordic Sustainable Construction Collaboration
- Vision: Most sustainable and circular construction sector globally
- Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) harmonization across 5 Nordic countries
- **Game-changer**: Reused products set to zero in environmental impact
- Digital Product Passports (DPP) enabling material traceability
- Strong focus on both renovation AND new construction

### 6. Greenland National Strategy
- **Trust-based approach**: No mandatory legislation
- "We don't have the control mechanisms of Denmark"
- Focus on voluntary adoption, education, and incentives
- Strategy formally approved in 2024, now in implementation
- Construction sector sustainability as national priority

### 7. Retrofit First Economics
- **Key statistic**: Renovation ≈ 50% cost of new construction in Greenland
- London's "retrofit first" regulatory approach transformed city development
- Oslo circular economy as driver for major city development projects
- Business case: Maintaining resources + creating societal relevance
- Economic viability enables circular principles scaling

### 8. Circular Procurement Leverage
- Municipalities hold enormous market-shaping power
- Public procurement as implementation tool for circular principles
- Riga example: Circular guidelines in all procurement practices
- Greenland integrating circularity into public tender requirements
- Pre-study requirements ensure circular thinking from project start

### 9. Urban-Rural Value Symbiosis
- Knowledge transfer between capital cities and regional municipalities
- Cesis (regional) learning from Riga (capital), adapting approaches
- Arctic (sparse) and Nordic (dense) contexts informing each other
- Small municipalities demonstrating adaptability and resourcefulness
- International collaboration enabling small regions to achieve more

### 10. Community & Trust Foundation
- "The most important thing you have to build when you build places is trust"
- Cesis memorandum: Social contract and public commitment
- Greenland's trust-based regulation vs. Denmark's control mechanisms
- Citizen engagement and sharing circular knowledge crucial
- Community centers (like Riga's urban resource center) as gathering places
